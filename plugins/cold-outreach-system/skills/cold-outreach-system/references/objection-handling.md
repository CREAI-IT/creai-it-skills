# Objection Handling & Reply Management

## The 4-Step Response Framework

For any objection, follow this structure:

```
1. ACKNOWLEDGE — Validate their response (don't argue)
2. EMPATHIZE — Show you understand their position
3. REFRAME — Offer a new perspective or address the concern
4. LOW-FRICTION NEXT STEP — Make it easy to continue (not a meeting)
```

## Common Objections & Response Scripts

### Objection 1: "Not interested"

**The instinct**: Move on. **The reality**: "Not interested" often means "not interested right now" or "you didn't give me a reason to be interested."

**Response A (Standard):**
```
Subject: Re: [thread]

Totally fair, [Name].

Out of curiosity — is it the timing, or does [problem] not resonate with what [company] is focused on right now?

Either way, no pressure. Just want to make sure I'm not missing something.

[Name]
```

**Response B (Softer — for senior executives):**
```
Subject: Re: [thread]

Appreciate the honesty, [Name]. I'll close this loop.

If [problem area] ever becomes a priority, happy to share what's working for [similar company type]. No follow-up from me otherwise.

[Name]
```

**Rules:**
- Never argue with "not interested"
- Ask ONE clarifying question — not to sell, but to learn
- If they say "not interested" twice, stop permanently

### Objection 2: "How did you get my email?"

**Response:**
```
Subject: Re: [thread]

Fair question. Your email is publicly available on [LinkedIn / company website / conference speaker list].

I reached out because [one-sentence reason tied to their specific context]. If this isn't relevant, I completely understand — happy to remove you. Just reply "stop."

[Name]
```

**Rules:**
- Be transparent about the source
- Immediately pivot to WHY you reached out (relevance)
- Always offer removal
- Never be defensive

### Objection 3: "Too expensive" / "No budget"

**Response:**
```
Subject: Re: [thread]

Makes sense, [Name]. Budget is always a factor.

Quick thought — [similar company] was spending [X hours/dollars] on [the problem] before switching. [Product] paid for itself in [timeframe] through [specific saving].

Would it help to see how the math works for a team like yours? Happy to share a quick breakdown — no commitment.

[Name]
```

**Rules:**
- Don't discount immediately (devalues your product)
- Reframe as ROI, not cost
- Use a specific customer example
- Offer to show the math, not to "get on a call"

### Objection 4: "We already use [Competitor]"

**Response:**
```
Subject: Re: [thread]

Good to know you're already solving for [problem area] — that tells me it's a priority.

Curious: on a scale of 1-10, how happy are you with [competitor] for [specific use case]?

Not trying to sell you a switch — just genuinely curious if [specific differentiator] matters for your workflow.

[Name]
```

**Rules:**
- Never trash the competitor
- Acknowledge they're already solving the problem (validates them)
- The 1-10 question is powerful — anything below 8 opens a conversation
- Focus on your unique differentiator, not general superiority

### Objection 5: "Send me more info"

**Response:**
```
Subject: Re: [thread]

Absolutely. Here's a quick summary:

[2-3 bullet points: core value prop, key differentiator, proof point]

[Link to relevant resource — case study, one-pager, or demo video]

What specifically would be most useful to see? Happy to tailor.

[Name]
```

**Rules:**
- Don't send a 20-page deck
- Send targeted, specific info (not your entire website)
- Ask what they care about (this keeps the conversation going)
- "Send me more info" can be a brush-off OR genuine interest — the ask question reveals which

### Objection 6: "Not the right time"

**Response:**
```
Subject: Re: [thread]

Totally understand, [Name]. Timing is everything.

When would be a better time to revisit? I'll set a reminder and check back — no spam in between.

[Name]
```

**Rules:**
- Respect the timing
- Get a specific future date if possible
- Set an actual reminder and follow up when they said to
- Don't follow up before the agreed time

### Objection 7: "Not the right person"

**Response:**
```
Subject: Re: [thread]

Appreciate you letting me know, [Name].

Who on your team handles [problem area]? Happy to reach out directly — and I'll mention you pointed me their way.

Thanks for the redirect.

[Name]
```

**Rules:**
- Thank them genuinely (referrals are gold)
- Ask for a specific person, not a department
- Mention their name when you reach out ("Sarah suggested I reach out")
- This is often the most valuable objection — internal referrals have 3-5x higher reply rates

## Reply Categories & Actions

When processing replies (especially in FULL_MODE with Gmail MCP), categorize each response:

### Category: Interested

**Signals**: "Tell me more", "Sure, let's chat", "How does it work?", "What's the pricing?"

**Action:**
- Respond within 2 hours (speed matters)
- Match their energy (if they're brief, be brief)
- Answer their specific question
- Propose a clear next step (demo, call, trial)
- Don't over-sell — they're already interested

### Category: Objection

**Signals**: "Not interested", "Too expensive", "Bad timing", "We use [competitor]"

**Action:**
- Use the relevant objection script above
- Respond within 24 hours
- One response per objection — don't debate
- If they object twice, mark as do-not-contact

### Category: Question

**Signals**: "How is this different from X?", "Does it integrate with Y?", "What about [specific concern]?"

**Action:**
- Answer the question directly and specifically
- Don't pivot to a pitch — answer first, then bridge
- Include a proof point if possible
- End with a soft CTA: "Does that address your concern?"

### Category: Out of Office

**Signals**: Auto-reply with return date

**Action:**
- Note the return date
- Pause the sequence for this prospect
- Resume 2 days after their return with a fresh, relevant email
- Don't send another automated email during their OOO

### Category: Unsubscribe

**Signals**: "Stop", "Remove me", "Unsubscribe", "Don't contact me"

**Action:**
- Remove immediately from all sequences
- Mark as do-not-contact permanently
- Send a brief confirmation: "Done — removed you from all future emails. Apologies for the inconvenience."
- Do NOT try to win them back

### Category: Negative/Angry

**Signals**: "This is spam", "Never email me again", hostile language

**Action:**
- Remove immediately
- Brief, professional response: "Apologies for the disruption. Removed you permanently. Won't happen again."
- Do NOT engage with hostility
- Review your targeting — multiple angry replies = ICP problem

## Follow-Up Timing Rules

| Situation | Wait | Action |
|-----------|------|--------|
| After "interested" reply | 0-2 hours | Respond immediately |
| After objection | 4-24 hours | Thoughtful, not reactive |
| After "not the right time" | When they specified | Set calendar reminder |
| After OOO | Return date + 2 days | Fresh email, new angle |
| After no reply to follow-up | 3-5 days | Next email in sequence |
| After breakup email (no reply) | 30-60 days | One final re-engagement (new angle only) |
| After "stop" / unsubscribe | Never | Permanent removal |

## Tone Rules for Replies

- **Match their length**: If they write 2 sentences, you write 2 sentences
- **Match their formality**: If they're casual, be casual
- **Never be defensive**: Even if they're rude
- **Never guilt-trip**: "I've sent you 5 emails..." is toxic
- **Be grateful**: Every reply — even negative — is a gift (most people just ignore)
- **Be direct**: Answer their question, then (and only then) bridge to next step
