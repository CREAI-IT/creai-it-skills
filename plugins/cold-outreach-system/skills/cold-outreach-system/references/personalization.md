# Personalization & Research Frameworks

## Why Personalization Matters

A personalized first line increases reply rates by 2-3x compared to generic outreach. The first line is the single most important element in a cold email — it determines whether the prospect reads on or deletes.

Personalization ≠ using their name. Real personalization shows you did research and understand their specific context.

## Levels of Personalization

| Level | What | Example | Reply Rate Impact |
|-------|------|---------|-------------------|
| 1. Name/Company | Just {{first_name}} and {{company}} | "Hi Sarah at Acme..." | Baseline |
| 2. Industry | Industry-specific pain point | "Most e-commerce teams struggle with..." | +20-30% |
| 3. Role-specific | Pain point for their job title | "As a Head of Eng, you're probably..." | +40-50% |
| 4. Company-specific | Something unique to their company | "Saw Acme just launched [feature]..." | +60-80% |
| 5. Trigger-based | Timely event at their company | "Congrats on the Series A last week..." | +100-150% |

**Rule**: Tier A prospects get Level 4-5. Tier B gets Level 3. Tier C gets Level 2.

## Trigger Events — The Highest-Converting Personalization

Trigger events are things that happened at the prospect's company that create a buying window. They're the #1 predictor of outreach success.

### Trigger Events Table

| Trigger Event | Where to Find | Response Window | First Line Template |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Funding round** | Crunchbase, LinkedIn, Google News | 2-8 weeks post-announcement | "Congrats on the [Series X]. With [growth plans], [problem] usually becomes critical fast." |
| **New executive hire** | LinkedIn, company blog | 0-4 weeks (they're looking to make changes) | "Saw you just joined [company] as [title] — when I've seen [similar transitions], [problem] is usually one of the first things that comes up." |
| **Product launch** | Product Hunt, company blog, Twitter | 1-4 weeks | "Just saw [company] launched [product] — impressive. Teams scaling after a launch usually hit [problem] around this stage." |
| **Job posting** | LinkedIn Jobs, Indeed | Active (while posting is live) | "Noticed [company] is hiring a [role] — that usually signals [challenge]. [Product] helps teams [solve that] without adding headcount." |
| **Acquisition/merger** | Google News, Crunchbase | 2-6 weeks | "Read about the [acquisition]. Integrating [systems] after a deal like this usually surfaces [problem]." |
| **Award/recognition** | Google News, LinkedIn | 1-2 weeks | "Congrats on [award] — well deserved. Curious if [related challenge] is on your radar as you scale." |
| **Technology change** | BuiltWith, job postings | Ongoing | "Noticed [company] is moving to [tech] — we've helped [similar companies] through that transition." |
| **Conference/event** | Event websites, LinkedIn | 1-2 weeks before/after | "Saw you're speaking at [event] on [topic] — [specific comment about their session]." |

### Trigger Event Stacking

Combine 2+ triggers for highest conversion:
- Funding + hiring = "Growing fast, need tools to scale"
- New exec + job postings = "Building a team, open to new approaches"
- Product launch + positive reviews = "Momentum, time to optimize"

## Research Process

### Step 1: LinkedIn Profile (2 minutes)

Find:
- Current role and how long they've been there
- Previous companies (shared history?)
- Recent posts or articles
- Mutual connections
- Skills and endorsements (what they care about)
- Recommendations (what others say about them)

### Step 2: Company Signals (3 minutes)

Find:
- Recent news (Google "[company name]" past month)
- Crunchbase profile (funding, team size, investors)
- Job postings (what they're hiring for = where they're investing)
- Blog/changelog (recent launches, product direction)
- Tech stack (BuiltWith, Wappalyzer, or job posting tech requirements)

### Step 3: Social Activity (2 minutes)

Find:
- Twitter/X posts (opinions, interests, hot takes)
- LinkedIn posts/comments (professional interests)
- Podcast appearances (interview quotes)
- Conference talks (speaking topics)

**Total research time per prospect: 5-7 minutes for Tier A. 2-3 minutes for Tier B.**

## First Line Formulas

### Formula 1: Trigger-Based
```
"[Congrats on / Saw / Noticed] [specific trigger event] — [your insight about what that means for them]."

Examples:
"Saw Acme just closed a $15M Series A — scaling the eng team that fast usually means CI pipelines become the bottleneck."
"Noticed you're hiring 3 SDRs — when outbound ramps up that fast, reply rates usually drop without a system."
```

### Formula 2: Content-Based
```
"Your [post/talk/article] on [topic] resonated — especially [specific point you found interesting]."

Examples:
"Your LinkedIn post about ditching Kubernetes was a great read — especially the cost comparison data."
"Your talk at SaaStr about PLG was spot on — the bit about activation rate thresholds was exactly what we're seeing too."
```

### Formula 3: Observation-Based
```
"Noticed [something specific about their company/product] — [your insight about what it means]."

Examples:
"Noticed Acme's pricing page just added an enterprise tier — that transition usually comes with a sales team scaling challenge."
"Saw your changelog has shipped 12 features this quarter — that velocity is impressive but usually strains QA processes."
```

### Formula 4: Mutual Connection
```
"[Mutual connection] mentioned you're working on [topic] — [brief connection to your value]."

Examples:
"Alex Kim mentioned you're rebuilding Acme's data pipeline — we helped his team at [company] cut that project from 6 months to 6 weeks."
```

### Formula 5: Tech Stack
```
"Noticed [company] uses [technology] — teams running [tech] usually run into [specific problem]."

Examples:
"Saw Acme runs on Postgres + Redis — teams at your scale usually hit query performance issues around the 10M row mark."
```

## Personalization at Scale

For Tier B prospects where full personalization isn't worth the time:

### Segmented Personalization
Group prospects by shared characteristics and personalize by segment:

```
Segment: "Series A B2B SaaS companies hiring engineers"

First line: "Saw [company] is growing the eng team post-Series A —
teams at this stage usually hit [problem] first."

→ Same structure, swap in {{company}} — feels personal, scales to 50+ prospects
```

### The 3-Pillar Scaling System

1. **Segmentation**: Group by industry + role + trigger
2. **Template per segment**: Write a first line template per segment
3. **Variable insertion**: Swap in company-specific details (company name, specific trigger, specific tech)

This gives you 80% of the personalization impact with 20% of the effort.

## What NOT to Personalize

| Don't Do This | Why |
|---|---|
| "I love your website design!" | Transparent flattery, feels fake |
| "I see you went to [University]!" | Creepy if it's not relevant |
| "I noticed your birthday was last week" | Definitely creepy |
| "As a fellow [generic group]..." | Not specific enough to count |
| "Great to connect!" (when you haven't connected) | Premature familiarity |

**Rule**: Personalization must be relevant to their professional context and connect to your value prop. If it doesn't connect to why you're reaching out, it's just small talk.
