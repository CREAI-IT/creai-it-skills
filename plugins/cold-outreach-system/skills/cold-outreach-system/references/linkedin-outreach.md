# LinkedIn Cold Outreach

## Platform Rules & Limits

### Connection Request Limits

| Account Type | Weekly Limit | Notes |
|-------------|-------------|-------|
| Free LinkedIn | 100-150/week | Reduced if acceptance rate is low |
| LinkedIn Premium | 150-200/week | Slightly more lenient |
| Sales Navigator | 200-250/week | Highest limit + advanced search |

### InMail Limits

| Account Type | Monthly InMails | Notes |
|-------------|----------------|-------|
| Premium Business | 15/month | Credited back if replied within 90 days |
| Sales Navigator Core | 50/month | Credited back if replied within 90 days |
| Recruiter Lite | 30/month | — |

### LinkedIn's Anti-Automation Stance

LinkedIn actively detects and penalizes automation:
- Account restrictions for suspicious activity
- Connection request throttling
- Temporary bans for mass messaging
- Permanent bans for repeated violations

**Rule**: Everything on LinkedIn should feel manual. No automation tools. No mass messaging. Personal, one-at-a-time outreach.

## Connection Request Strategy

### The Golden Rule

A connection request is NOT a pitch. It's an introduction. The goal is to get accepted, not to sell.

### Connection Request Templates (Under 300 Characters)

**Template 1: Shared Interest**
```
Hi [Name] — saw your post on [topic]. I'm working on something similar in the [space] and would love to connect with people thinking about this.

— [Your name]
```

**Template 2: Mutual Connection**
```
Hi [Name] — [Mutual connection] and I were discussing [topic] and your name came up. Would love to connect.

— [Your name]
```

**Template 3: Genuine Compliment**
```
Hi [Name] — your [article/talk/post] on [specific topic] was one of the best takes I've read on this. Would love to follow your work more closely.

— [Your name]
```

**Template 4: Industry Peer**
```
Hi [Name] — fellow [role/industry] here. Building [brief description] and always looking to connect with others in the space.

— [Your name]
```

### Connection Request Rules

- **Under 300 characters** (the limit)
- **Never pitch in the connection request** — instant reject
- **Reference something specific** — shows you're not mass-requesting
- **No links** in the connection request
- **No "I'd love to pick your brain"** — this is a take, not a give
- **Accept rate goal**: > 40% (below this, your requests are too generic)

## Follow-Up DM Strategy

### Timing

- Wait 24-48 hours after connection acceptance
- Don't send a DM the second they accept (looks automated)
- If they accepted but didn't respond to your request note, wait 48 hours

### DM Sequence

**DM 1: Thank + Warm Intro (Day 2 after acceptance)**
```
Thanks for connecting, [Name].

[One sentence referencing why you connected — their post, shared interest, mutual connection]

Quick question — [question related to their work that's genuinely interesting to you].

No pitch, just curious.
```

**DM 2: Value Share (Day 5-7)**
```
[Name] — thought of you when I saw this [article/data/resource].

[Brief explanation of why it's relevant to them]

[Link or insight]

No response needed — just thought it'd be useful.
```

**DM 3: Soft Intro to Your Offer (Day 10-14)**
```
[Name] — one more thought.

I've been working on [your product/service] and we're helping [similar companies/people] with [specific outcome].

Given what you're doing at [company], thought it might be relevant. Worth a look?

Either way, no pressure — enjoy the connection.
```

### DM Rules

- **One DM per interaction** — don't send 3 messages in a row
- **Wait for response windows** — if they don't reply to DM 1, still try DM 2 (value add)
- **If no reply after DM 3** — stop. Don't keep messaging.
- **If they reply negatively** — thank them and stop
- **Mirror their response length** — short reply = short follow-up
- **Never send voice notes cold** — only after established rapport
- **Never send video cold** — only after they've shown interest

## Content Engagement (Warm-Up Strategy)

The highest-converting LinkedIn outreach isn't DMs — it's commenting on their content first.

### The Warm-First Approach

**Week 1**: Like and comment on 2-3 of their posts
- Comments should be thoughtful (not "Great post!")
- Add your perspective, ask a question, share a related experience
- 3-5 sentences minimum for comments that get noticed

**Week 2**: Send connection request
- Reference one of their posts you commented on
- They'll recognize your name from their notification feed

**Week 3**: Follow up with DM sequence
- By now, you're not a stranger — you're someone who engages with their content
- Reply rates from warm outreach: 20-30% (vs. 5-10% for cold DMs)

### Comment Templates

**Template 1: Agree + Extend**
```
Couldn't agree more on [specific point]. We've seen the same pattern at [your company] —
specifically with [related example]. The part that often gets overlooked is [your additional insight].
```

**Template 2: Respectful Pushback**
```
Interesting take. I've seen this work for [context], but in [different context],
the opposite seems true because [reason]. Would love to hear your thoughts on that nuance.
```

**Template 3: Data Addition**
```
This tracks with what we're seeing. [Specific data point] from our experience with
[number] of [similar companies]. The [specific aspect] was especially true.
```

## LinkedIn Outreach for Different Goals

### Goal: Book a Demo/Call

| Step | Action | Timing |
|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Engage with their content (2-3 comments) | Week 1 |
| 2 | Send connection request (no pitch) | Week 2 |
| 3 | DM 1: Thank + warm intro | Day 2 after acceptance |
| 4 | DM 2: Value share (resource/insight) | Day 5-7 |
| 5 | DM 3: Soft intro + interest CTA | Day 10-14 |
| 6 | If interested: propose specific call time | Within 2 hours of reply |

### Goal: Partnership/Collaboration

```
Hi [Name] — I run [your product], and we serve a similar audience to [their product].

I've been thinking about a potential [integration / co-marketing / content collaboration]
that could benefit both our users.

Would you be open to exploring this? I have a few specific ideas.
```

### Goal: Content Collaboration

```
Hi [Name] — your content on [topic] consistently resonates with my audience.

I'm putting together a [roundup / guest post / podcast / webinar] on [specific topic]
and think your perspective would add a lot of value.

Would you be interested? Happy to share details.
```

## LinkedIn Content + Outreach Synergy

Your LinkedIn posts work as a passive outreach channel:

- **Post content** that your target ICP cares about
- **Prospects see your content** before you DM them (instant credibility)
- **Content acts as proof** — your expertise is visible
- **DM converts higher** because they already know your thinking

The best LinkedIn outreach combines:
1. Consistent content posting (3-5x/week)
2. Strategic engagement on prospect content
3. Personalized connection requests
4. Value-first DM sequences
