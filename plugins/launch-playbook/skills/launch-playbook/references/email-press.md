# Email & Press Launch Strategy

## Email Launch Sequences

### Pre-Launch Email Sequence (3 emails)

#### Email 1: Announcement (T-14)

**Subject line options:**
- "Something new is coming — and you're first to know"
- "I've been building something for [audience] like you"
- "[First name], early access to [Product]?"

**Body:**
```
Hey [First name],

I've been working on something for the past [timeframe] and wanted
to share it with you first.

[One paragraph: what the product does + who it's for]

[One paragraph: why you built it — personal story, keep it real]

I'm launching [date/timeframe]. As someone who [reason they're on
this list], I'd love for you to be among the first to try it.

Reply to this email if you want early access — I'll send you a
link before the public launch.

[Your name]

P.S. [One-line teaser about a specific feature or benefit]
```

#### Email 2: Build Anticipation (T-5)

**Subject line options:**
- "5 days until launch — here's what [Product] does"
- "[First name], sneak peek inside [Product]"
- "The problem I couldn't stop thinking about"

**Body:**
```
Hey [First name],

Quick update on [Product] — launching in 5 days.

Here's what it does:

🔹 [Benefit 1 — specific outcome, not feature]
🔹 [Benefit 2 — specific outcome]
🔹 [Benefit 3 — specific outcome]

[One paragraph: social proof or early user feedback if available]

On launch day, I'll send you the link + an exclusive offer
for early supporters.

In the meantime, here's a quick peek:
[Screenshot or GIF link]

[Your name]
```

#### Email 3: Launch Day

**Subject line options:**
- "🚀 [Product] is LIVE — your exclusive link inside"
- "It's here. [Product] is officially live."
- "[First name], [Product] just launched — grab your [discount/access]"

**Body:**
```
Hey [First name],

[Product] is officially live! 🎉

[Direct link to Product Hunt / landing page]

As a thank you for being an early supporter, here's your
exclusive offer: [specific discount/bonus/extended trial]

Three things I'd love from you today:

1. Check out [Product] and let me know what you think
2. If you find it useful, leaving a comment on our
   [Product Hunt page / landing page] would mean a lot
3. Share with anyone who might benefit

[One paragraph: quick reminder of what the product does
and the key benefit]

I'll be online all day responding to questions and feedback.
Hit reply anytime.

Thank you for your support — it genuinely means everything.

[Your name]

P.S. [Urgency element — "Launch pricing ends [date]" or
"First 100 users get [bonus]"]
```

### Post-Launch Follow-Up (T+3)

**Subject line options:**
- "Thank you + what's next for [Product]"
- "[Product] launch results (+ a surprise)"
- "We hit [milestone] — here's what I learned"

**Body:**
```
Hey [First name],

Quick update after our launch:

📊 [Metric 1 — upvotes, signups, traffic]
💬 [Metric 2 — comments, feedback received]
🏆 [Achievement — PH ranking, press coverage]

[One paragraph: gratitude + what you learned]

[One paragraph: what's coming next — roadmap, features, improvements]

If you haven't tried [Product] yet, here's the link:
[URL]

[If applicable: "Your launch discount is still active until [date]"]

Thanks again for being part of this.

[Your name]
```

### Subject Line Formulas

**High-performing patterns:**
- **Curiosity**: "Something I've been working on secretly..."
- **Specificity**: "[Product] helped [user] save 12 hours/week"
- **Personal**: "[First name], I built this because of you"
- **Urgency**: "24 hours left: [Offer description]"
- **Question**: "Tired of [specific pain]?"
- **Number**: "3 reasons I built [Product]"

**Rules:**
- Keep under 50 characters (mobile truncation)
- Use first name personalization
- Avoid spam triggers: "FREE", "ACT NOW", all caps, excessive punctuation
- Test 2-3 subject lines per send (A/B test)
- Preheader text should complement, not repeat, the subject line

### Email Best Practices
- **Send time**: Tuesday-Thursday, 9-11 AM recipient's local time
- **From name**: Use personal name (not company name) for launch emails
- **Format**: Plain text or minimal HTML. Avoid image-heavy templates for launch emails.
- **Length**: 150-250 words max. Mobile-first.
- **Single CTA**: One clear call to action per email.
- **Reply-friendly**: Encourage replies. Replies improve deliverability.

## Press & Media Outreach

### When Press Works (and When It Doesn't)

**Press is high-impact for:**
- Products with a novel angle or contrarian story
- Products solving a problem journalists/editors personally understand
- Products with impressive traction or social proof
- Products in trending topics (AI, climate, remote work)

**Press is low-impact for:**
- Very niche B2B tools (journalists can't relate)
- Products without a story beyond "we built a thing"
- Products in crowded categories without clear differentiation
- Very early-stage products with no traction

### Finding Journalists

**Method 1: Reverse-engineer competitor coverage**
- Search "[competitor name] launch" or "[competitor name] TechCrunch/TheVerge"
- Note which journalists covered them
- These journalists cover your category

**Method 2: Platform-specific search**
- Search the publication's site for your keywords
- Check journalist Twitter/X profiles for topic interests
- Look for "DMs open" or email in bio

**Method 3: Tools**
- Twitter/X search: "[topic] journalist" or "[topic] reporter"
- LinkedIn: search for "[publication] reporter" + filter by topic
- Press page on competitor websites often lists coverage

### Journalist Pitch Email

**Subject line:**
```
[Category]-specific angle — not "Check out my startup"

Good: "AI tool that cuts landing page creation from 2 weeks to 2 hours"
Bad: "Exciting new AI startup launch"
```

**Body (under 200 words):**
```
Hi [First name],

I saw your piece on [specific article they wrote] — [one sentence
showing you actually read it and connecting it to your product].

I'm launching [Product] on [date], and I think it's relevant to
your coverage of [their beat].

The short version: [One sentence — what it does + for whom]

What's different: [One sentence — the unique angle]

Traction: [One line — users, revenue, notable customers, or
growth metric]

[Optional: one line about the founder's story if compelling]

Would you be interested in [specific ask: early access,
a quick demo, an embargoed briefing]?

I can share more details, screenshots, and a press kit if helpful.

Best,
[Your name]
[Title, Company]
[Phone number]
```

**Rules:**
- **Personalize every email.** Reference a specific article they wrote.
- **Lead with the story**, not the product.
- **Be concise.** Journalists get 100+ pitches/day.
- **Include the ask** (demo, embargo, early access).
- **Follow up once** after 3-5 days. Do not follow up more than twice total.
- **Send Tuesday-Thursday, 7-9 AM** their local time.
- **Never mass-email with BCC.** Always individual, personalized emails.

### Press Kit Components

Prepare a press kit page or shared folder with:

1. **One-pager** — Product name, tagline, description (50 words), key features (3-5), founder bio, contact info
2. **High-res images** — Product screenshots (3-5), founder headshot, logo (PNG + SVG, light and dark backgrounds)
3. **Fact sheet** — Launch date, pricing, target audience, key metrics, technology stack
4. **Founder bio** — 100-word bio with relevant background and photo
5. **Quotes** — 2-3 pre-written quotes from the founder (journalists will use these directly)
6. **Demo video** — 60-90 second product walkthrough (optional but high-impact)

### Embargo Strategy

An **embargo** means you share information with journalists before your public launch, with the agreement that they won't publish until a specific date/time.

**When to use embargoes:**
- You want multiple publications to cover you on the same day
- You have a big enough story to warrant it
- You have relationships with specific journalists

**How embargoes work:**
1. Pitch journalists 2-3 weeks before launch
2. Offer exclusive or embargoed access
3. Clearly state the embargo date/time in writing
4. Provide all press kit materials during embargo period
5. On launch day, all coverage goes live simultaneously

**Warning:** Only use embargoes if you have a genuinely newsworthy launch. Most indie product launches don't need or benefit from embargoes.

### Alternative PR Channels

If traditional press isn't a fit:

| Channel | Best for | How |
|---|---|---|
| **Newsletter sponsorships** | Reaching niche audiences | Pay for placement in relevant newsletters (TLDR, Morning Brew, etc.) |
| **Podcast appearances** | Building founder credibility | Pitch indie business/tech podcasts as a guest |
| **YouTube reviews** | Products with visual appeal | Send early access to relevant YouTubers |
| **Blog/newsletter features** | Indie/startup audience | Reach out to indie hackers, solopreneurs with newsletters |
| **Indie Hackers** | Bootstrapped/indie products | Post a detailed launch story with transparent metrics |
