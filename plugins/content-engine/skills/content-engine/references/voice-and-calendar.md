# Voice Calibration & Content Calendar

## Voice Profile System

### Why Voice Matters

AI-generated content fails when it sounds like AI. The Content Engine's output must sound like the founder — their vocabulary, their sentence patterns, their humor, their stance. A voice profile captures these patterns and enforces them across all outputs.

### Voice Calibration Process

**Step 1: Collect Samples**

Ask the user for 2-3 pieces of content they've written and liked. Ideal samples:
- A social media post that performed well
- A blog post or newsletter they're proud of
- An email or message that represents their natural voice

**Step 2: Analyze Dimensions**

Evaluate each sample across these dimensions:

| Dimension | Spectrum | Example Indicators |
|-----------|----------|-------------------|
| **Sentence length** | Short/punchy ← → Flowing/narrative | "Ship fast. Break things." vs. "The key to building a successful product lies in understanding that speed matters more than perfection." |
| **Vocabulary register** | Casual ← → Technical ← → Academic | "This is a game" vs. "This leverages the flywheel effect" vs. "This exhibits network externalities" |
| **Humor style** | None ← → Dry ← → Self-deprecating ← → Pop culture | No jokes vs. deadpan vs. "I completely screwed this up" vs. "It was giving main character energy" |
| **Opening patterns** | Question ← → Statement ← → Story ← → Data | "Ever wonder..." vs. "Here's the truth:" vs. "Last Tuesday..." vs. "73% of..." |
| **Stance** | Balanced ← → Opinionated ← → Provocative | "There are many approaches" vs. "This is the right way" vs. "Everyone is wrong about this" |
| **Perspective** | First person ← → Second person ← → Third person | "I found that..." vs. "You should..." vs. "Founders who..." |
| **Formality** | Conversational ← → Professional ← → Formal | "Tbh, this slaps" vs. "This approach works well" vs. "This methodology yields results" |
| **Paragraph length** | Ultra-short (1-2 lines) ← → Medium (3-4 lines) ← → Long | LinkedIn bro-etry vs. standard vs. essay style |

**Step 3: Produce Voice Profile**

Generate a 5-7 bullet point voice profile. Example:

```
Voice Profile — [Founder Name]:
• Short, punchy sentences (avg. 8-12 words). Rarely exceeds 20 words.
• Casual-technical register — uses industry terms but explains them simply.
• Dry humor, occasional self-deprecation ("I spent 3 months building a feature nobody wanted").
• Opens with bold statements or questions, never with context-setting.
• Strongly opinionated — takes a clear stance and defends it.
• First person ("I" / "we") dominant. Uses "you" for direct advice.
• No emoji in body text. Rare use in CTAs or sign-offs.
```

**Step 4: Confirm with User**

Present the profile and ask: "Does this sound like you? What would you adjust?"

### Voice Profile Application Rules

When applying the voice profile to content:

1. **Match sentence length patterns** — if they write short, don't pad
2. **Use their vocabulary** — if they say "ship", don't change to "deploy"
3. **Match their humor** — if they're not funny, don't add jokes
4. **Match their opening style** — if they always open with a question, do the same
5. **Respect their stance** — if they're opinionated, don't soften their takes
6. **Never add AI mannerisms** — no "Let me explain", "Here's the thing", "Buckle up"

### Voice Consistency Check

Before delivering any output, verify:
- Does this sound like something [founder name] would actually post?
- Would their existing followers notice a change in voice?
- Are there any phrases that feel "AI-generated" vs. "human-written"?
- Does the energy level match their natural style?

## Content Calendar & Drip Scheduling

### Drip Principle

Never publish all content adaptations on the same day. Reasons:
- Audiences on different platforms don't fully overlap — dripping maximizes unique reach
- Same-day multi-platform posting looks automated and inauthentic
- Spacing content creates sustained visibility over days/weeks
- You can test and iterate — if a Tuesday tweet performs well, adapt the approach for Thursday's LinkedIn post

### Drip Schedule Template

For a single source piece atomized across multiple platforms:

```
| Day | Platform | Content Type | Atoms Used | Optimal Time | Notes |
|-----|----------|-------------|------------|--------------|-------|
| Mon | Twitter/X | Hook tweet (single) | #1 (Stat) | 8-9am ET | Test the strongest atom |
| Tue | LinkedIn | Story post | #2 (Story) + #7 (Before/After) | 7-8am local | Expand with personal context |
| Wed | — | Rest day | — | — | Avoid content fatigue |
| Thu | Twitter/X | Thread (5-8 tweets) | #3 (Framework) + #5 (How-To) | 9-10am ET | Full thread with depth |
| Fri | Instagram | Carousel (8 slides) | #3 (Framework) | 11am-12pm | Visual adaptation |
| Mon+1 | Newsletter | Deep dive | #2 + #8 (Case Study) | 7am local | Synthesize for subscribers |
| Tue+1 | Naver Blog | Korean adaptation | #3 + #5 | 10am KST | Full rewrite for Korean |
```

### Content Calendar Planning

#### Weekly Content Volume (Recommended for Solo Founders)

| Platform | Posts/Week | Time Investment |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Twitter/X | 5-7 (mix of singles + 1 thread) | 2-3 hours |
| LinkedIn | 2-3 | 1-2 hours |
| Instagram | 3-4 (mix of posts + reels) | 2-3 hours |
| Blog | 1 pillar post | 3-4 hours |
| Newsletter | 1 | 1-2 hours |
| Korean platforms | 1-2 (Naver Blog + KakaoTalk) | 1-2 hours |

**Total with Content Engine**: 10-16 hours/week → 3-5 hours/week (the engine does the heavy lifting)

#### Monthly Content Pyramid

```
Week 1: Pillar Content (blog post or deep-dive thread)
  → Atomize → Schedule atoms across Week 1-2

Week 2: Derivative Content (LinkedIn posts, newsletter, carousel)
  → Continue dripping Week 1 atoms + add new derivatives

Week 3: New Pillar Content OR expand a Week 1-2 winner
  → Bidirectional flow: high-performing atoms → new pillar

Week 4: Recycling + Planning
  → Refresh and repost top performers from 60+ days ago
  → Plan next month's content pillars
```

## Content Recycling Protocol

### Recycling Categories

| Category | Description | Recycle After | How to Refresh |
|----------|-------------|---------------|----------------|
| **Evergreen** | Frameworks, principles, how-tos | 60-90 days | Update stats, add new examples |
| **Seasonal** | Holiday, year-end, conference-related | Annually | Update year, refresh examples |
| **One-time** | News reactions, timely takes | Never | Archive permanently |
| **Winners** | Top 10% performing content | 45-60 days | Repost with minor tweaks |

### Recycling Rules

1. **Never repost identical content** — always change the hook, update a stat, or add a new angle
2. **Minimum 60-day gap** for similar content on the same platform
3. **Cross-platform recycling is fine** — your Twitter audience ≠ your LinkedIn audience
4. **Track what you've recycled** — avoid repeating the same content to the same audience
5. **Stop recycling after 3 rotations** — by then, extract a new angle or let it rest

### Refresh Techniques

| Technique | What to Change | When to Use |
|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| **New hook** | Change the opening line/question | Most common refresh |
| **Updated stat** | Replace old data with newer numbers | Stat-based content |
| **New example** | Swap case study or illustration | Framework/how-to content |
| **Format swap** | Tweet → Thread, or Post → Carousel | Cross-format testing |
| **Angle shift** | Same topic, different perspective | High-performing topics |
| **Platform migrate** | Move winning Twitter content to LinkedIn | Cross-platform expansion |

### Winner Identification & Expansion

**Engagement signals that identify winners:**

| Signal | Threshold | Action |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Save/bookmark rate | > 3x your average | Expand to long-form |
| Reply quality | Thoughtful responses, personal stories | Create follow-up content |
| DM triggers | "Can you write more about this?" | Pillar content topic |
| Share/RT ratio | > 2x your average | Repost with variations |
| Cross-platform pickup | Others reference your content | Authority content — expand |

**Winner expansion flow:**
1. Identify the winner (single tweet, LinkedIn post, carousel)
2. Ask: "What's the bigger idea behind this?"
3. Expand into Tier 1 pillar content (blog, thread, newsletter)
4. Atomize the new pillar content for another round of distribution

## Cross-Platform Analytics Reference

### Key Metrics per Platform

| Platform | Primary Metric | Secondary | Vanity (Ignore) |
|----------|---------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Twitter/X | Bookmarks, Replies | Retweets, Profile visits | Impressions |
| LinkedIn | Comments, Dwell time | Profile visits | Likes |
| Instagram | Saves, Shares | Reach, Profile visits | Likes |
| Blog | Time on page, Scroll depth | Organic traffic | Page views |
| Newsletter | Reply rate, Click rate | Open rate | List size |
| YouTube Shorts | Avg. view duration, Subscribers gained | Comments | Views |
| TikTok | Completion rate, Shares | Followers gained | Views |
| Reddit | Upvote ratio, Comment quality | Karma | — |
| HN | Points, Comment quality | Front page time | — |

### Content Performance Review Cycle

**Weekly** (15 min):
- Which posts outperformed? Why?
- Which underperformed? Why?
- Any winners worth expanding?

**Monthly** (30 min):
- Top 5 performing pieces across all platforms
- Content pillar performance (which topics resonate?)
- Platform health (any declining engagement trends?)
- Plan next month's content pillars

**Quarterly** (1 hour):
- Overall content strategy review
- Platform mix optimization (drop underperforming platforms?)
- Voice profile update (has your voice evolved?)
- Recycling review (what's ready to rotate?)
