# Social Platforms — Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Instagram, Threads

## Twitter/X

### Platform Character

- Fast, opinionated, punchy
- Algorithm rewards engagement (replies > retweets > likes)
- Threads outperform single tweets for reach (3-5x)
- Controversial takes and hot takes drive engagement
- Value-packed threads drive saves and follows

### Format Specs

| Format | Length | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Single tweet | Max 280 chars (free), 25,000 (Premium) | Aim for 200-240 chars for readability |
| Thread | 3-15 tweets | Sweet spot: 5-8 tweets. Each must stand alone |
| Image | 1200x675px (16:9) or 1080x1080px (1:1) | Single image gets 150% more engagement |
| Video | Max 2:20 (free), 60min (Premium) | Under 60 seconds performs best |
| Poll | 2-4 options, 24hr-7day duration | Drives 2-3x engagement vs. regular tweets |

### Tweet Thread Formula

```
Tweet 1 (Hook): Bold claim, surprising stat, or provocative question
                — This tweet decides if anyone reads the rest
                — No "Thread 🧵" or "[1/10]" (outdated)
                — End with a curiosity gap or incomplete thought

Tweet 2-N (Body): One idea per tweet. Structure options:
  - Numbered list (most scannable)
  - Story progression (most engaging)
  - Framework steps (most saveable)

Each body tweet:
  - Opens with the key point (don't bury it)
  - Uses line breaks for readability
  - Can stand alone if someone sees only that tweet
  - Avoids filler transitions between tweets

Final Tweet (CTA): Soft CTA options:
  - "If this was useful, follow @handle for more on [topic]"
  - "What would you add to this list?"
  - "Repost if someone in your network needs this"
  — NEVER: "Like and retweet!" or "Follow for more content!"
```

### Single Tweet Patterns

**Pattern 1: Stat + Insight**
```
[Surprising stat].

[What it actually means for you].
```

**Pattern 2: Contrarian Take**
```
Unpopular opinion: [contrarian statement].

Here's why: [1-2 sentence explanation].
```

**Pattern 3: Before/After**
```
Before [product/method]: [pain state]
After [product/method]: [desired state]

The difference? [Key insight].
```

**Pattern 4: Question Hook**
```
What if [provocative reframe]?

Most people think [common belief].
But [surprising reality].
```

**Pattern 5: Listicle**
```
[N] things I learned [doing X]:

1. [Lesson] — [brief explanation]
2. [Lesson] — [brief explanation]
...

The biggest one? #[N].
```

### Twitter Algorithm Signals (2025-2026)

- **Replies** are weighted highest (10x likes)
- **Bookmarks** are a strong signal (indicates value)
- **Dwell time** matters (longer content read = higher rank)
- **External links** are deprioritized (put links in replies, not main tweet)
- **Images** boost reach ~150%
- **First 30 minutes** are critical (engagement velocity determines distribution)

### Posting Times

- Best: Tue-Thu, 8-10am ET or 12-1pm ET
- Good: Mon/Fri, 9-11am ET
- Avoid: Sat-Sun (30-50% lower reach unless your audience is global)

### Hashtag Strategy

- Maximum 2 hashtags per tweet (more looks spammy)
- Use niche hashtags, not broad ones (#IndieHackers > #startup)
- Place at end of tweet, never in the main text
- Twitter is de-emphasizing hashtags — quality content matters more

## LinkedIn

### Platform Character

- Professional but increasingly personal and story-driven
- Algorithm rewards long-form text posts with engagement
- "Bro-etry" (short lines, lots of line breaks) still works but fatigue is growing
- Carousels (PDF documents) get highest reach
- Video is growing but text posts still dominate

### Format Specs

| Format | Length | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Text post | Max 3,000 chars | Sweet spot: 1,200-1,800 chars (150-250 words) |
| Article | Unlimited | Lower distribution than posts, higher SEO value |
| Carousel (PDF) | Up to 300 slides, 1080x1350px | Sweet spot: 8-12 slides |
| Image | 1200x627px (landscape) or 1080x1350px (portrait) | Portrait gets more feed real estate |
| Video | Max 10min | Under 90 seconds performs best |
| Poll | 2-4 options, 1-2 week duration | High engagement but low-quality engagement |

### LinkedIn Post Formula

```
Line 1 (Hook): Stops the scroll. Must work in the preview (first 2-3 lines).
  — Bold statement, surprising outcome, or personal vulnerability
  — NO: "I'm excited to announce..." or "Thrilled to share..."

Line 2-3: Context — set up the story or insight.
  — Brief, not detailed. Save depth for the middle.

Middle (Body): The substance. Structure options:
  — Story format: "Here's what happened..."
  — List format: "3 things I learned..."
  — Framework format: "My approach to [X]..."

  Use short paragraphs (1-2 sentences each).
  Use line breaks between paragraphs.
  Bold key phrases sparingly (**like this**).

Closing (CTA): Conversation starter, NOT promotion.
  — "What's been your experience with [topic]?"
  — "Agree or disagree?"
  — "What would you add?"
  — NEVER: "Like this post if you agree" or "Share with your network!"
```

### LinkedIn Carousel Format

```
Slide 1 (Cover): Bold title + subtitle. Visual hook.
  — Large text, high contrast, clean design
  — Include your name/handle subtly

Slide 2 (Problem): Why this matters. Pain point or curiosity gap.

Slides 3-10 (Content): One idea per slide.
  — Large text (minimum 24pt equivalent)
  — Max 30-40 words per slide
  — Use consistent visual style throughout

Slide 11 (Summary): Key takeaways in 3-5 bullets.

Slide 12 (CTA): "Follow [name] for more" + profile info.
  — Don't ask to "like and share"
```

### LinkedIn Algorithm Signals (2025-2026)

- **Dwell time** is the #1 signal (longer content read = higher rank)
- **Comments** weighted 5x more than likes
- **First hour** is critical (engagement velocity)
- **External links** reduce reach by 40-50% (put in first comment)
- **Carousels** get 2-3x reach vs. text posts
- **Engagement pods** are penalized — genuine engagement only
- **Native content** (created on LinkedIn) outperforms shared content
- **"See more" clicks** are a positive signal (use hooks that make people expand)

### Posting Times

- Best: Tue-Thu, 7-8am local time (catches morning commute)
- Good: Mon-Fri, 12-1pm (lunch break)
- Avoid: Weekends (50-70% lower reach)
- Post frequency: 3-5x per week maximum (more causes follower fatigue)

### LinkedIn Content Types by Goal

| Goal | Best Format | Why |
|------|-------------|-----|
| Reach/impressions | Carousel | Highest distribution |
| Engagement/comments | Personal story post | Emotional connection drives responses |
| Authority/expertise | Framework post | Showcases thinking |
| Lead generation | Value post + link in comments | Don't sacrifice reach for clicks |
| Recruitment/employer brand | Behind-the-scenes post | Humanizes the company |

## Instagram

### Platform Character

- Visual-first, increasingly content-driven (Reels, carousels)
- Carousels have highest save rate and reach
- Reels still dominate for discovery
- Static images declining unless visually striking
- Stories for engagement, Feed for discovery

### Format Specs

| Format | Specs | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Feed post (square) | 1080x1080px | Classic, works everywhere |
| Feed post (portrait) | 1080x1350px (4:5) | Maximum feed real estate |
| Carousel | 1080x1350px, up to 20 slides | Sweet spot: 5-10 slides |
| Reel | 1080x1920px (9:16), 15-90s | Under 30s for discovery |
| Story | 1080x1920px (9:16) | 24hr lifespan, engagement tool |

### Instagram Carousel Template

```
Slide 1 (Hook): Bold, scannable headline
  — Problem statement or surprising claim
  — Large text, minimal design
  — Must work as a standalone image in the feed

Slides 2-8 (Content): One insight per slide
  — Consistent design system (colors, fonts, layout)
  — Mix text-heavy and visual slides
  — Use arrows or "swipe" indicators subtly

Slide 9 (Summary): Key takeaways

Slide 10 (CTA):
  — "Save this for later" (save rate is Instagram's #1 signal)
  — "Share with someone who needs this"
  — Follow prompt with handle
```

### Caption Formula

```
Line 1 (Hook): First line visible without expanding. Must stop scroll.

Body: 3-5 short paragraphs.
  — Value-driven (teach, inspire, or entertain)
  — Use line breaks liberally
  — Emojis sparingly as bullet points (✓ not 🎉🚀💡)

CTA: Single ask.
  — "Save this" or "Share this" or "Drop a [emoji] if..."

Hashtags: 3-5 relevant hashtags.
  — Mix niche and medium-volume
  — Don't use 30 hashtags (looks desperate, algorithm ignores)
  — Place after a line break, below the CTA
```

### Instagram Algorithm Signals (2025-2026)

- **Saves** are the strongest signal (3x weight of likes)
- **Shares** (DM sends) ranked second
- **Carousel completion rate** (how many slides swiped) matters
- **Reel completion rate** determines distribution
- **Consistency** matters more than frequency (3-4x/week > daily posting that burns out)

## Threads

### Platform Character

- Conversational, text-first (like early Twitter)
- Lower competition = higher organic reach (2026 window)
- Algorithm rewards conversations, not broadcasts
- No hashtags system (keyword-based discovery)
- Cross-posts from Twitter don't perform well — native content wins

### Format Specs

| Format | Specs | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Post | Max 500 chars | Shorter posts tend to perform better |
| Image | 1080x1080px | Single image support |
| Carousel | Up to 20 images | Similar to Instagram |
| Video | Up to 5 min | Short clips preferred |

### Threads Post Patterns

**Conversational start**: "Anyone else notice that [observation]?"
**Hot take**: "[Contrarian statement]. And I'll explain why."
**Question + Answer**: "How do you [problem]? Here's what works for me: [solution]"
**Quick tip**: "Tip for [audience]: [actionable advice in 1-2 sentences]."

### Threads vs. Twitter

| Aspect | Threads | Twitter/X |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Tone | Warmer, more conversational | Sharper, more opinionated |
| Format | Shorter text, fewer threads | Threads and long-form |
| Discovery | Feed algorithm, no hashtags | Hashtags + search + For You |
| Links | Not penalized | Deprioritized |
| Competition | Lower (early platform) | Higher |
| B2B audience | Smaller | Larger |
